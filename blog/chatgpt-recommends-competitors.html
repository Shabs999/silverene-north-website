Is ChatGPT Recommending Your Competitors Instead of You?

🕐 7 min read 📅 Feb 2026 ✍️ Silverene North Corp

We tested 50 local businesses to see which ones ChatGPT recommends — and the results were surprising. Find out what separates the recommended from the overlooked.

The Experiment

We selected 50 local businesses across Toronto and Dubai spanning five industries: marketing agencies, restaurants, law firms, real estate brokerages, and dental clinics. For each business, we asked ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google Gemini variations of the same question: "What's the best [business type] in [city]?"

Then we cross-referenced the AI recommendations against the actual businesses we tested. The results were eye-opening.

The Invisible Businesses: What Went Wrong

The 62% of businesses that were never mentioned by AI tools weren't doing anything wrong per se — they just hadn't optimized for the AI era. Their websites were functional. They had some reviews. But when AI tried to build a mental model of their business, it didn't have enough clear, consistent, authoritative information to work with.

Common Patterns Among the Invisible

  • Incomplete or outdated Google Business Profiles — missing categories, no business description, outdated hours
  • No structured data — websites that looked great but gave AI systems no machine-readable information about the business
  • No external citations — the businesses existed only on their own website and maybe a couple of social profiles
  • Thin or generic content — websites that said "we provide great service" but never specified what, for whom, or where
  • Review gaps — fewer than 20 reviews, often with no owner responses

The Core Problem

AI tools can only recommend what they can confidently understand. If your digital presence is thin, inconsistent, or vague — the AI simply moves on to a competitor it understands better. You don't get penalized. You get ignored.

Industry Breakdown: Who's Most at Risk?

Not all industries face equal risk. Our testing revealed that some sectors are significantly more vulnerable to AI invisibility than others.

High Risk: Service Businesses with Generic Names

Businesses with common names ("City Dental", "Pro Legal Services") struggled most. AI tools couldn't differentiate them from hundreds of similar businesses unless they had strong external citation profiles.

Moderate Risk: Established Businesses That Haven't Updated

Businesses that were well-established 5+ years ago but hadn't updated their digital presence for the AI era. They had old SEO done well but zero structured data and no recent content.

Lower Risk: Businesses With Active Content Strategies

Businesses that regularly published helpful content, responded to reviews, and maintained updated profiles performed significantly better — even when they were smaller and less established than competitors.

The 3 Fastest Fixes

If you want to quickly improve your chances of being recommended by AI tools, focus on these three actions first:

  1. Complete your Google Business Profile today. Fill in every field. Add photos. Write a detailed business description that includes what you do, who you serve, and where you're located. This single action had the highest correlation with AI recommendation in our testing.
  2. Add Schema.org markup to your website. At minimum, add LocalBusiness and Organization schema. This gives AI systems structured, machine-readable information about your business that dramatically improves how well they understand you.
  3. Get featured in at least 3 external sources. A local news mention, an industry directory listing, or a guest article can dramatically improve your citation profile. AI tools weight external mentions heavily as trust signals.

What This Means for Your Business

The businesses being recommended by AI today are building a compounding advantage. Every month they're visible, they get more citations, more mentions, more reviews — reinforcing their AI visibility further. The businesses that wait are falling further behind.

The good news: it's not too late. Unlike Google SEO, which can take a year to show results, AI visibility improvements can take effect in weeks. The landscape is still being established, and there's a significant opportunity right now for businesses that move quickly.
